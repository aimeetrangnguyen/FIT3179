Instant ramen ratings in Asia

Instant ramen or instant noodle is one of the most common and popular fast-foods around the world, especially in Asia. It was invented by Momofuku Ando , who is the founder of Nissin Foods in Japan. Nissin is also the first brand to introduce instant ramen under cup packaging design. Ramen noodle is loved by many people because of its convenience and cheap price. In each country and region, the flavour of ramen could change accordingly to the local taste. In this visualisation, we will explore the quality (stars rating) of instant ramen in Asia based on regions, style of packaging and flavours.

Overall ratings

The average ratings of ramen ranges from around 3.2 to 4.2 across the countries in Asia. The majority of them fall in the range of 3.2 - 3.6 stars . Vietnam is the one with the lowest ramen ratings. This is understandable since Vietnamese prefers rice noodle such as Pho or vermicelli. It's interesting to see that Japan - where the instant ramen is originated from, only got 3.98 average stars ratings. The value is actually higher compared to the general but it's still below Indonesia, Malaysia and Cambodia - those countries that achieve more than 4 stars.

The different styles of ramen

There are five styles of ramen which are: pack, bowl, cup, tray and box. Even though the first product of instant ramen came under cup style, pack style has become more and more popular and now it turns out to be the most produced ramen packaging style. The main advantage of pack over bowl or cup is that the package is more compact and it uses less plastic. Normally, the bowl and cup style would have a plastic fork attached with it and also an extra clear plastic wrap for protection. Thus, pack ramen is normally cheaper than bowl and cup ramen. Although box style has the least amount of production but box ramen gets the maximum stars in both Japan and Malaysia.

Singapore gets the highest average ratings for pack style while Malaysia ranks number one in ratings for bowl style. The cup style has the highest rating in Myanmar and Japan achieves that highest rating for tray style.

Most popular flavours of ramen

Top 10 most flavours of ramen that have been produced are: spicy, chicken, beef, seafood, pork, shrimp, sesame, tonkotsu and vegetarian. China, Hong Kong, Taiwan and South Korea are the four countries in Asia where many of their cuisines taste spicy. Thus, no wonder there is a massive production of spicy ramen.
